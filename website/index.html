About me

Data Analyst -> Software Engineer

I grew up in Nepal, where my family could not afford higher education. Fortunately, in high school I learned that I could attend certain colleges in the U.S. on a full scholarship. We didn’t have internet access at home, but I started regularly commuting to a library where I could go online and complete applications, research visas, and apply for financial aid.

Attending college in the U.S. changed my life. It vastly expanded my educational opportunities and altered my perspectives. I studied mathematics and economics, allowing me to work in data science post-college. I enjoyed this work, but soon realized the importance of being able to code if I wanted to work on advanced data science projects.

In 2018, I began to study Python in my free time. I loved it, but could not pursue it as I wished. I began considering CS graduate programs. I want shift from data science to software engineering, as I am attracted to building softwares. I now see my future in the U.S., where I hope to make a career in tech—an industry that is almost nonexistent in Nepal.
